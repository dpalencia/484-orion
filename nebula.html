The Orion Nebula
is one of the most photographed objects in the night sky,
and it's easy to see why.

At a distance of 1300 lightyears, the Orion Nebula is one of the brightest nebulae. It is visible to the naked eye, and is situated underneath Orion's belt making it easy to find. The Orion Nebula has given us insights into how star systems are formed from clouds of gas and dust, allowing us to learn more about how our own solar system came to be.
