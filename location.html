Note: Orion is not visible year round!
Orion is only visible in the night sky from November to February. These are the months in which Orion is in the sky in the nighttime. The rest of the year, Orion is washed out by the sun in the daytime sky.

Where to Find Orion

Because of Orion's location relative to other prominent landmarks and its own bright and prominent stars, Orion is easy to find. Using these tips, finding Orion in the night sky should be easy.

  1. Look along the ecliptic.
    One easy technique to locate Orion is to follow the Ecliptic. The Ecliptic is the path the Sun, Moon, and planets trace along the sky as they follow their orbits.

  2. Look for Sirius.
    Another landmark to watch for is Sirius. Sirius is the brightest satar in the northern hemisphere, so it is easy to spot. Sirius is also along the ecliptic, and Orion is right next to it. Trace a path from sirious eastward along the ecliptic, and Orion will be right there.

  3. Look for Orion's Belt.
    Orion's Belt is also hard to miss. Look for the distinct row of three bright stars and you've found Orion. Flanking Orion's belt, you should see Betelgeuse and its telltale red color and Rigel with its own blue hue.
